Pranab Das

Senior Research Fellow, National University of Singapore.

Hello! I am Pranab. Welcome to my homepage. I am a senior research fellow at the Singapore Synchrotron Light Source, National University of Singapore. I am working in the field of experimental Condensed Matter Physics. I am interested in studying electronic structure of novel and functional materials by spin- and angle-resolved photoemission spectroscopy (ARPES). We are working on several types of materials including topological insulators, Weyl semimetals, transition metal dichalcogeniges, and transition metal oxides. We also specialize on material growth (single crystals and thin films) and first principles calculations in collaboration with other research groups. On the surface preparation part, single crystals are cleaved or exfoliated in ultra high vacuum, or sputtered and annealed, while thin films are de-capped (ex-situ grown) or grown in-situ by Pulsed LASER Deposition. For more details about our work, please visit research and publications pages. You can also find my CV here. If you are interested in collaborating with us, please drop me an email.

I also try writing codes to make things a little better. You can find my open-source projects on GitHub. You may also check out my notes and tutorials, which are still work in progress. I like reading, bit of photography. I enjoy hiking, walking, or just being out in the nature.
