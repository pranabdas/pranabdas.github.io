Pranab Das

Research Fellow, National University of Singapore.

Hello! I am Pranab Kumar Das. I am a Research Fellow at the Singapore Synchrotron Light source, National University of Singapore. I am working in the field of experimental Condensed Matter Physics. I am interested in studying electronic structure of novel and functional materials by spin- and angle-resolved photoemission spectroscopy (ARPES). We are working on several types of materials including topological insulators, Weyl semimetals, transition metal dichalcogeniges, and transition metal oxides. We also specialize on material growth (single crystals and thin films) and first principles calculations in collaboration with other research groups. On surface preparation, single crystals are cleaved or exfoliated in ultra high vacuum, or sputter and annealed, while thin films are de-capped (ex-situ grown) or grown in-situ by Pulsed LASER Deposition (PLD). For more details, visit my research and publications page. You can also find my CV here. If you are interested in working with us, please drop me an email.
