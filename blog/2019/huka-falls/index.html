Huka falls

August 12, 2019   New Zealand 

Huka falls, Taupō, New Zealand.

Perhaps, you have heard about how kind the Kiwis are. Here is one of my first hand experiences. We spend this morning in Craters of Moon National Park. Later we planned to walk from there to Huka falls. The weather was really volatile. When we were about to leave the park, it started pouring. So we decided to call a taxi from the city. While we were waiting, a park employee came to us and asked where we were heading. He told he finished his shift and going back to the city, so if we want he can give us a ride. We said but we are going to another point, he said that is fine, he would drop us there. But we confirmed the taxi, and the driver perhaps already left from the city. He insisted it is absolutely fine to cancel. We called back to cancel, nobody argued or asked for penalty :P So we got on his car, he told us some stories along the way. He dropped us, wished good luck, and we could never thank him enough.

Few more clicks on our way back to Lake Taupō along the river.
